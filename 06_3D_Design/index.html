Assignment 6: 3D Design & Printing

Use a photogrammetry app to create a 3D scan of something and design/prepare to print something on the 3D printer.

3D Photogrammetry Scanning

Qlone

I used the app Qlone to complete my 3D scans. With this app, you needed to print out a checkerboard-like image, called the QLONE Mat and place your object on top of the board. This helps the program recognize where the object is in space as you rotate around it. As you rotate around the object on the mat, an AR dome shows up on your screen. You must scan the object from every direction in a hemisphere around it and small pieces of the dome will dissappear as you complete that area of the scan.

The results varied from some very high quality scans to some very poor representations. Overall, the app did a great job of 3D scanning objects that weren't shiny and had fairly regular patterns. The app also gave an option to merge multiple scans of the same object (taken at different angles) to get a more accurate scan, although for me, this process always failed to merge correctly and only enhanced the scan of the moonsnail shell.

3D Printing

Fusion 360 Design

For my 3D printed design, I wanted to create a semi-mobile shark model. To make it move, I wanted to create multiple interlocking pieces. Each piece would have a vertical cylindrical hole through it. The next piece in the chain would connect to it through a cylindrical insert. Normally, these would be able to slide though each other in the vertical direction, but because this design will be 3D printed, we can use additive methods to permantly fix each piece in its place. This will give it some freedom to flex, while retaining its general form. The Fusion 360 model is available to download below.

Print Formating

With the Fusion 360, 3D model complete, it was time to save the shark as a file ready to be 3D printed. To do so, I had to transfer it to the Prusa Slicer Software and get it ready to be sliced. This included adding support material and other specifications. After the shark was ready to be printed, it was clear it would require a large amount of support material. This was expected, but as I have never used a 3D printer before, I feel this may still be a feasible design (it just may take a while to remove all the support material).
