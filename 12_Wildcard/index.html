Assignment 12: Wildcard / Project Prep

Mold and cast and interesting pattern and work on your final project.

Creating the Mold

In order to create my mold, I first had to decide on what I wanted to create a mold of. I wanted something that had an interesting pattern, so I ultimately settled on a scallop shell. However, one scallop shell is extremely thin and would not make a great cast, so I decided to glue to scallop shells together. I had to do this very carfully in order to ensure there were no cracks that the liquid silicon could leak into the middle of the shell. In oder to affix the shell to the bottom of a container for molding I decided to glue the shell to a flat rock that could be used as a stand. I then glued this directly to the bottom of a plastic mixing cup. After mixing both silicon parts A and B together, I slowly poured it over the shell, making sure I had minimal bubbles in the mixture. After letting the mixture dry for a day, I removed the cup from the outside and cut the mold in half (I had previously marked the long edge of the shell on the cup in oder to ensure I would get a cut down the middle of the mold). My cut went right through the center of the scallop shell as intended. I removed the shell and was left with a perfect scallop shell mold.

Casting

Once the mold was created, the casting was very simple and straightforward. I pressed the two-piece mold tightly together and used rubber bands to hold it. I then filled the mold with water and put it in the freezer for some time. Removing the mold some time later left a perfect ice casting of the scallop shell!
